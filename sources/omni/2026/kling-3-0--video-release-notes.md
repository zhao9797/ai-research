## Kling VIDEO 3.0 Upgrade

Building on Kling VIDEO O1 and Kling VIDEO 2.6, the new model integrates multiple tasks — such as Text-to-Video, Image-to-Video, Reference-to-Video, video content addition and removal, video modification and transformation — into a unified, native multimodal training model. This model not only supports longer video generation (up to 15 seconds), but also offers Native Audio, more flexibility in storyboarding and shot control, and higher accuracy in prompt adherence. Overall image realism is significantly enhanced, with characters' performances becoming more expressive and dynamic. Based on the upgraded unified multimodal approach, Kling VIDEO 2.6 has been upgraded to VIDEO 3.0, while Kling VIDEO O1 has been upgraded to VIDEO 3.0 Omni, bringing a comprehensive evolution in creative control and narrative possibility.

## VIDEO 3.0 Model Technical Approaches

1) Native Framework for Multi-Task, All-Purpose Use Cases: The VIDEO 3.0 model completely reconfigures the underlying architecture and data pipeline, natively supporting in-depth analysis of multimodal prompts and cross-task integration. By introducing a unified multimodal prompt formatting solution, the model can accurately understand complex logic in narratives, supporting longer video output, flexible shot control, and better prompt adherence. 

2) Native Cross-Modal Audio Engine: Building upon existing capabilities, with optimal noise sampling intervals across different modalities, and a new module for audio extraction and embedding, the model is able to output more natural and coherent sound effects, dialogues, and singing performances. Paired with an upgraded end-to-end prompt reference system, it achieves breakthroughs in voice preservation and precise prompt references, enabling deep audio-visual coherence.

3) Multimodal Reference and Decoupling Control Solution: The model supports subject building based on video reference, and adding specific voices to subjects. By utilizing feature decoupling and recombination technologies, the model allows for adding or editing subjects across difference scenes with complexity and flexibility, ensuring seamless integration of subjects with audio-visual features throughout long video creation.
