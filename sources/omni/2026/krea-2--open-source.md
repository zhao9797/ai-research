## Questions about Krea 2 Open-Source

What is Krea 2 Open-Source?

 

Krea 2 Open-Source is the open-source release of Krea 2. It ships as two checkpoints: Krea 2 RAW for training and research, and Krea 2 Turbo for fast, high-quality text-to-image inference.

What is the difference between RAW and Turbo?

 

Krea 2 RAW is the undistilled base checkpoint. It is diverse, malleable, and intended for fine-tuning, post-training, and LoRA training. Krea 2 Turbo is an 8-step distilled checkpoint for fast inference.

Which checkpoint should I train LoRAs on?

 

Train LoRAs on Krea 2 RAW, then apply them on Krea 2 Turbo. LoRAs trained on RAW are designed to express strongly on Turbo, so you can train on the base and run inference fast.

Should I use RAW for final image generation?

 

Use RAW when you want a malleable base checkpoint for fine-tuning or research. For polished, fast text-to-image outputs, use Krea 2 Turbo.

How many steps does Krea 2 Turbo use?

 

Krea 2 Turbo is an 8-step distilled checkpoint built for fast, high-quality text-to-image generation.

What architecture does Krea 2 Open-Source use?

 

Krea 2 Open-Source uses a Qwen Image VAE, a 12B dense DiT backbone, and a Qwen3-VL text encoder with multi-layer feature aggregation.
