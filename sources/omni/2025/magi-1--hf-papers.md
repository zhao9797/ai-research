## Abstract

MAGI-1 generates temporally consistent and scalable videos through autoregressive prediction of video chunks, enabling real-time, memory-efficient deployment and controllable generation.

Generated by [Qwen/Qwen2.5-Coder-32B-Instruct](/Qwen/Qwen2.5-Coder-32B-Instruct)

We present MAGI-1, a world model that generates videos by [autoregressive](/papers?q=autoregressive)ly
predicting a sequence of [video chunks](/papers?q=video%20chunks), defined as fixed-length segments of
consecutive frames. Trained to denoise per-chunk noise that increases
monotonically over time, MAGI-1 enables [causal temporal modeling](/papers?q=causal%20temporal%20modeling) and naturally
supports [streaming generation](/papers?q=streaming%20generation). It achieves strong performance on [image-to-video](/papers?q=image-to-video)
(I2V) tasks conditioned on [text instructions](/papers?q=text%20instructions), providing high temporal
consistency and scalability, which are made possible by several algorithmic
innovations and a dedicated infrastructure stack. MAGI-1 facilitates
controllable generation via [chunk-wise prompting](/papers?q=chunk-wise%20prompting) and supports real-time,
memory-efficient deployment by maintaining constant peak inference cost,
regardless of video length. The largest variant of MAGI-1 comprises 24 billion
parameters and supports context lengths of up to 4 million tokens,
demonstrating the scalability and robustness of our approach. The code and
models are available at https://github.com/SandAI-org/MAGI-1 and
https://github.com/SandAI-org/[MagiAttention](/papers?q=MagiAttention). The product can be accessed at
https://sand.ai.
