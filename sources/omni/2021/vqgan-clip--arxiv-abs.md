# Title:VQGAN-CLIP: Open Domain Image Generation and Editing with Natural Language Guidance

Authors:[Katherine Crowson](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Crowson,+K), [Stella Biderman](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Biderman,+S), [Daniel Kornis](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Kornis,+D), [Dashiell Stander](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Stander,+D), [Eric Hallahan](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Hallahan,+E), [Louis Castricato](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Castricato,+L), [Edward Raff](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Raff,+E)

View a PDF of the paper titled VQGAN-CLIP: Open Domain Image Generation and Editing with Natural Language Guidance, by Katherine Crowson and Stella Biderman and Daniel Kornis and Dashiell Stander and Eric Hallahan and Louis Castricato and Edward Raff

[View PDF](/pdf/2204.08583)
> Abstract:Generating and editing images from open domain text prompts is a challenging task that heretofore has required expensive and specially trained models. We demonstrate a novel methodology for both tasks which is capable of producing images of high visual quality from text prompts of significant semantic complexity without any training by using a multimodal encoder to guide image generations. We demonstrate on a variety of tasks how using CLIP [37] to guide VQGAN [11] produces higher visual quality outputs than prior, less flexible approaches like DALL-E [38], GLIDE [33] and Open-Edit [24], despite not being trained for the tasks presented. Our code is available in a public repository.
