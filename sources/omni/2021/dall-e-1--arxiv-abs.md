# Title:Zero-Shot Text-to-Image Generation

Authors:[Aditya Ramesh](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Ramesh,+A), [Mikhail Pavlov](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Pavlov,+M), [Gabriel Goh](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Goh,+G), [Scott Gray](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Gray,+S), [Chelsea Voss](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Voss,+C), [Alec Radford](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Radford,+A), [Mark Chen](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Chen,+M), [Ilya Sutskever](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Sutskever,+I)

View a PDF of the paper titled Zero-Shot Text-to-Image Generation, by Aditya Ramesh and 7 other authors

[View PDF](/pdf/2102.12092)
> Abstract:Text-to-image generation has traditionally focused on finding better modeling assumptions for training on a fixed dataset. These assumptions might involve complex architectures, auxiliary losses, or side information such as object part labels or segmentation masks supplied during training. We describe a simple approach for this task based on a transformer that autoregressively models the text and image tokens as a single stream of data. With sufficient data and scale, our approach is competitive with previous domain-specific models when evaluated in a zero-shot fashion.
